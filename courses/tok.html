What is the tok assessment in IB Diploma?

TOK alongwith Extended Essay contribute to a maximum of 3 points out of 45 in the IB final result where the rest of 42 points come from the 6 opted subjects. Unlike other subjects that are graded on a point scale of 3-7, TOK and EE are first graded in terms of A/B/C/D which are then converted into 0-3 point scale.

TOK comprises two assessments namely the tok presentation(33% weightage) and the tok essay (67% weightage).

TOK Presentation

This is an internal assessment which means that most schools will complete this within the first year of IB. This is internally marked by the school teacher.

What is the format of tok presentation?

  • Each student is required to prepare a 10 minute presentation to be delivered in front of the teacher. However, adequate planning and feedback is provided prior to the assessment date.
  • Students chose a real life situation (RLS), something that is both recent and controversial in terms of its polarization of the public opinion. This can be an event or breakthrough publicized in a formal publication.
  • Students then have to formulate a Knowledge question that captures the essence of their RLS. A knowledge question should transform the real life occurrence into a more sophisticated and intellectual query through the use of TOK terms for e.g. AOKs , WOKs etc.
  • The main body of the presentation comprises claim and counterclaim answering the KQ.
  • A claim must be explored through various theoretical and anecdotal evidence and its analysis. Students can use concepts from the subjects they are taking in IB or step outside and use advanced research papers. In order to secure a high mark, students must ground their analysis in tok terminology, referencing to ways of knowing whenever possible.

TOK Essay

This assessment contributes to ⅔ of Tok grade and is externally marked. IB gives out a list of 6 essay prompts each year from which the students have to choose one and write a 1600 words essay

What is the format of TOK ESSAY?

  • Intro: Define Key Terms
  • Area of Knowledge 1:
    • Claim + Example
    • Counterclaim + Example
  • Area of Knowledge 2:
    • Claim + Example
    • Counterclaim + Example
  • Conclusion
