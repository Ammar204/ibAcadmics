The new IB economics syllabus : What's new?

If you are going to sit for the 2022 IB examinations, you will be going through a completely brand new topic breakdown and structure of the econ syllabus as per revised by the IBO. We compared the previous and the updated versions to highlight some of the key differences followed by some tips for how to approach it in the best way possible .

Introduction to Economics

The IBO has now classified the ‘intro to econ’ section as one of the units. This was previously only taught as a preface to the micro and macro unit. There will be a total of 10 teaching hours where you’ll basically go through simple questions like ‘what is economics?’ and ‘how do economists approach the world?’ We recommend you to take this unit seriously as the first assessment for this will define your predicted grade. Apart from grades it will be hugely beneficial to grasp those basic principles as they apply to most of the complex theory in the following units.

Microeconomics

This is probably the first extensive unit where the teacher will spend upto 35 teaching hours for SL, 70 hours for HL. Students will go over concepts like supply, demand, government intervention, and market failure. Most schools cover micro in the first year of dp which means your first year mock will be primarily based on this. We recommend you to go through certain exam style variations of the questions as they are used by most teachers.

Macroeconomics

This is where most students struggle to apply the theoretical concepts into real world scenarios involving governmental decisions. You will need to engage with these ideas with an open mind as you will be required to explore them in a long form essay.
The teacher is expected to spend upto 40 teaching hours for SL, 70 hours for HL where you’ll study measures of economic activity, macroeconomic objectives, demand-side policies, and supply-side policies.

The Global Economy

Previously this unit was broken down into international and development but now the IBO has put it as one single topic.
45 teaching hours for SL, 65 hours for HL where you’ll study the benefits of international trade, protectionism, exchange rates, balance of payments, sustainable development, and barriers/strategies to development
