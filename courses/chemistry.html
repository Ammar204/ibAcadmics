What is the aim of IB Chemistry?

Students must familiarize themselves with the basic quantities and calculation tools when looking at a chemical process. IB Chemistry can be divided into theoretical concepts, problems related to chemical reactions and practical experimentation. Once the students develop understanding of concepts in each topic, they are expected to employ mathematical and thinking skills to solve problems, calculate quantities and predict outcomes of experiments.

Why is IB chemistry challenging?

The concepts and numerous equations can be very confusing to contextualize and memorise. Also, many students struggle to convert values and quantities in a formula to its other forms. It can be a skill requiring depth of understanding and teachers have the job to not only provide explanations but to demonstrate solutions and various strategies for tricky problems in exam questions.

IB CHEMISTRY SL AND HL CORE

Both IB CHEM HL & SL syllabus include the first 11 topics. However, HL additionally includes topics 12-21. For the optional section, both HL & SL require you to choose one from the four optional topics A-D but HL will require you to cover a few additional sub-topics from the optional chapter that you choose..
