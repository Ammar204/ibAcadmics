IB English Paper 2

What is the aim of IB English Paper 2?

This paper requires students to show understanding of how and why is literature produced. When looking at the books, students should not see them merely as stories but try to ask bigger questions about their need for existence. Students should aim to develop an insight into the thought processes of poets, playwrights and novelists, asking questions about writers’ life growing up and what motivated them to write about particular matters. Students should also value the power of these works as reflection of social issues and drivers of change. Finally, the students should narrow down each idea and associate literary evidence to it from within the books.

Why is Paper 2 hard to master?

Students of literature and everyone else have only dealt with literature as means to indulge and entertain from narratives of constructed characters. It is neither natural nor comfortable to investigate details of every single aspect of authors life, society/culture of the time the book was written, key themes, characters and setting. All of these aspects require thorough research, hours of meticulous note taking and memorization of dozens of pages of such notes to acquire sufficient knowledge that will lead to a high scoring paper 2 essay.
